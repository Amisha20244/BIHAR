🎉 Famous Festivals of Bihar

A vibrant celebration of culture, devotion, tradition, and unity

🌞 Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is the soul of Bihar. It’s dedicated to Surya Dev and Chhathi Maiya. Women perform tough fasts and offer prayers to the rising and setting sun, standing in rivers. This festival shows the purity, discipline, and faith of Bihari culture.

Know More

👩‍👧‍👦 Jitiya

Jitiya is a fasting festival where mothers do 'nirjala vrat' for their children’s long life. It includes singing folk songs and prayers. This festival reflects the love, sacrifice, and power of motherhood in Bihar.

Know More

🌈 Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, is full of fun in Bihar. People throw gulal, sing Bhojpuri songs, dance, and enjoy sweets like gujiya. It’s about joy, spring, and the win of good over evil.

Know More

✨ Deepawali

Diwali is a festival of lights. People light diyas, do Lakshmi Puja, make rangoli, and celebrate with sweets and fireworks. It brings joy and hope across homes in Bihar.

Know More

📚 Saraswati Puja

On this day, students worship Goddess Saraswati – the goddess of knowledge. Books, pens, and instruments are offered to her. It's a major event in schools and colleges across Bihar.

Know More

🕊️ Sama-Chakeva

A special festival from Mithilanchal where sisters make clay birds and sing folk songs by the river for their brothers. It's a festival of love and justice.

Know More

🪁 Makar Sankranti

This harvest festival is celebrated by eating dahi-chura and tilkut, flying kites, and taking holy dips in rivers. It marks a fresh start in Bihar’s farming life.

Know More

🎶 Rajgir Mahotsav

A grand cultural festival with classical music, folk dance, and drama. Artists from all over India gather in Rajgir to show Bihar's beautiful heritage.

Know More

🐘 Sonepur Mela

Asia’s biggest cattle fair, with animals, cultural shows, food stalls, and rural crafts. It’s a mix of devotion, trade, and fun in one big mela.

Know More

🚣‍♀️ Bihula-Bishari

This folk festival from Bhagalpur tells the story of Bihula’s love and bravery to save her husband from snakebite. People sing, float boats, and pray to the snake goddess Mansa Devi.

Know More

🌼 Madhushravani

Madhushravani is celebrated by newly married women in Mithila. It includes 13 days of rituals, snake worship, folk songs, and stories that blend nature, love, and devotion.

Know More

🕉️ Buddha Purnima

Buddha Purnima is celebrated in Bodh Gaya, Bihar, to mark the birth, enlightenment, and death of Lord Buddha. Devotees offer prayers, meditate, and chant Buddhist scriptures.
